Who We Are

Women's Action for Self Reliance (WASR) is a community-based organisation dedicated to improving the lives of women and girls in Dowa, Malawi and surrounding communities.

Our Story

Founded in 2020, WASR emerged from the recognition that women in rural Malawi faced multiple and overlapping barriers to wellbeing — poverty, limited education, gender-based violence, and exclusion from decision-making.

Over more than two decades, we have grown from a small group of passionate women in Dowa into a recognised organisation that reaches hundreds of women and girls across the Central Region of Malawi.

Our approach is grounded in respect for local knowledge, community ownership, and a belief that women themselves hold the solutions to their challenges when given the right support and resources.

Core Values

  • Dignity and respect for all women and girls
  • Community ownership and participatory approach
  • Transparency and accountability in all we do
  • Solidarity — leaving no woman behind
  • Sustainability — building long-lasting change
  • Integrity and ethical practice
🎯

Our Mission

To empower women and girls in Dowa and Central Malawi through education, economic support, health advocacy, and rights-based programming for sustainable self-reliance.

🌟

Our Vision

A Malawi where every woman and girl lives with dignity, equal rights, and the freedom to realise her full potential.

🏛️

Our Approach

We work at the intersection of community mobilisation, skills development, and policy advocacy. Every programme is designed with the women we serve — not for them — ensuring relevance, ownership and sustainability across generations.

Our Programs

WASR delivers a range of integrated programmes that address the root causes of gender inequality and build lasting capacity in women and communities.

01

Women's Economic Empowerment Programme

This flagship programme equips women with vocational and entrepreneurial skills, links them to microfinance opportunities, and supports the formation of women savings groups (VSLAs). Participants gain practical skills in tailoring, food processing, soap making, and small business management.

Skills Training Microfinance Livelihoods
02

Girls' Education & Retention Initiative

Addressing high school dropout rates among girls in Dowa, this initiative provides scholastic materials, mentorship, and safe spaces for girls. We engage parents and community leaders to challenge norms that keep girls out of school, including early marriage and harmful practices.

Education Girl Child Mentorship
03

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) Prevention & Response

WASR trains community volunteers and local leaders as GBV champions. We operate referral pathways connecting survivors to legal, medical, and psychosocial support. Community dialogues challenge harmful gender norms and build zero-tolerance cultures within villages.

GBV Prevention Survivor Support Community Dialogue
04

Health, Nutrition & Reproductive Rights

Working closely with local health facilities, WASR conducts community health education campaigns on maternal health, family planning, HIV/AIDS prevention, and child nutrition. Women's health clubs serve as platforms for peer-to-peer learning and mutual support.

Health Education Nutrition Reproductive Rights
